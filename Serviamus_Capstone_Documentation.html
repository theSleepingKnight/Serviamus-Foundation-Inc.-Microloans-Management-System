Serviamus Foundation Inc.

Advanced Microloans Management System

Capstone Project Documentation & User Procedures Manual

Project Vision: To empower local microfinance officers with a digital ecosystem that eliminates manual paper trails, enhances data accuracy, and streamlines the collection process through a "Paper-Lite" operational model.

1. Executive Summary

The Serviamus Microloans Management System is designed for non-technical business users and technical evaluators alike. It bridges the gap between traditional manual tallying and modern digital efficiency. By centralizing customer records and automating complex loan logic, the system ensures that the Foundation can focus on community growth rather than technical bottlenecks.

2. System Objectives

  • Digital Transformation: Replacing physical ledger books with an immutable digital database.
  • Operational Speed: Reducing group collection processing time via specialized batch modes.
  • Business Intelligence: Providing management with real-time projected revenue and portfolio health metrics.

4. Procedures Manual (User Guide)

Step 1: Daily Monitoring
Staff should begin by reviewing the Daily Metrics on the dashboard. Pay close attention to "Receipts Issued" to ensure all physical collections from the field are encoded into the system.
Step 2: Member Onboarding & Verification
When a new member applies, they are added via the "Register New Customer" button in the Customers module. Once added, their profile can be verified at any time using the "View Profile" button (See Figure 3).
Step 3: Loan Processing & Archiving
1. Access the Loans module.
2. Assign new members to relevant Barangay groups.
3. Once a group has completed its repayment cycle (Weeks 1-25), use the Archive Group button. This ghosts the group card and changes its status, preventing accidental new charges while keeping the data for the Year-End report.
Step 4: Field Collection Entry (Batch Mode)
1. Navigate to Payments.
2. Toggle to "Group Batch" mode.
3. Open the Payment Sheet for the specific route group.
4. Enter the amounts collected as recorded in the field notebooks.
5. Verify the "Total to Process" matches the actual cash on hand before clicking Confirm Batch.
Step 5: System Maintenance
Administrators can update interest rates, CBU requirements, and penalties via the **Settings** gear icon. Changes are reflected in all new loan applications immediately.
Figure 8: Administrative Fee and Parameter Configuration.

5. Technical Highlights

The system is built as a Single Page Application (SPA), ensuring that users never have to wait for page reloads. This is critical for field entries where efficiency is paramount. The use of React's Context API ensures that as soon as a payment is made in the "Collection Desk," the "Dashboard" analytics update instantly without human intervention.
